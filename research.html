Research Projects

Research Engineer - NASA Cryogenic Vortex Generator (UW Applied Physics Laboratory)
At the UW Applied Physics Lab, I’m investigating vortex generator designs for cryogenic pipe systems under NASA funding. The goal is to improve thermal transfer by disrupting the Leidenfrost vapor barrier in cryogenic environments. I analyze various flow disruption geometries to balance reduced boil-off with minimal pressure drop, contributing to more efficient cryogen transfer line and rocket fuel systems.

Research Assistant - UW Fluids Lab
In this lab, I developed a magnet-controlled particle releaser with eight compartments to support experimental studies of flow dynamics. I integrated camera and laser tracking to monitor over 125 experimental runs and processed velocity field data using MATLAB and PIVmat. My system allowed for controlled fluid releases, enabling precise Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV) analysis.

Automated Single-Cell Printing - UW Allbritton Lab
I developed an automated calibration and motion-control system for a single-cell printing platform integrating a motorized Z-axis, XY microscope stage, and camera-based alignment. The goal was to transform a manual, operator-dependent spotting process into a repeatable and safe automated workflow. I built a Python (Tkinter) GUI to guide users through calibration, capturing critical parameters such as safe, in-well, and chip Z-heights, as well as stage positions for well and pen alignment. These values were validated, unit-consistent, and passed into a threaded automation sequence that coordinated multi-axis motion without blocking the user interface. The resulting system improved repeatability, reduced user error, and enabled reliable automated spotting with real-time status feedback and abort safety. This project strengthened my experience in laboratory robotics, hardware-software integration, calibration pipelines, and robust automation design.
